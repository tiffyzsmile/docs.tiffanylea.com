# Logarithms

## What are they?

- Answers the question:
  - How many of one number do we multiply to get another number?

## Example 1

How many 2s do we multiply to get 8?

```
2 × 2 × 2 = 8
```

We write "the number of 2s we need to multiply to get 8 is 3" as:

<Code>
  log<sub>2</sub>(8) = 3
</Code>

<img src="/public/images/logarithm-concept-1.svg" alt="logarithm-concept-1" />

The number we multiply is called the "base", so we can say:

- the logarithm of 8 with base 2 is 3
- log base 2 of 8 is 3
- the base-2 log of 8 is 3

## Example 2

<Code>
  log<sub>5</sub>(625) = ?
</Code>

<Code>5 × 5 × 5 × 5 = 625</Code>

<Code>
  log<sub>5</sub>(625) = 4
</Code>

## Example 3

<Code>
  log<sub>2</sub>(64) = ?
</Code>

<Code>2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 64</Code>

<Code>
  log<sub>2</sub>(64) = 6
</Code>
